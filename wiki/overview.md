# Course Overview: Generative AI for Graphics & Multimedia

The course curriculum moves from foundational theory to highly specialized, complex domains, always using the core concept of learning the underlying data distribution $p(\mathbf{x})$ as its engine.

## Fundamentals (Modules 1-3)
We begin by establishing techniques to learn latent representations:
1.  **Generative Models**: Comparing the theoretical rigor of Normalizing Flows against the empirical success of VAEs and Diffusion models.
2.  **Foundational Architectures**: Mastering the Transformer and Attention mechanism, which is necessary for both sequential data (text) and abstract representation learning.

## Specialized Applications (Modules 4-6)
We expand the utility of generative ideals into concrete domains:
1.  **3D Geometry**: Moving beyond 2D images to model volumetric and geometric data (Voxels, Meshes, NeRFs).
2.  **Deep Theory**: Incorporating advanced mathematical concepts like Bayesian inference to quantify model uncertainty and ensuring robustness.
3.  **Decision Making**: Applying AI theory to dynamic systems using Reinforcement Learning (RL), which focuses on finding optimal policies over time.

*This overview guides the student through the chronological and logical roadmap of the entire course.*
