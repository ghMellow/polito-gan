## **Loss function: alternatives** 

**Mean Squared Error (MSE)** 

- measures the average squared pixel-wise difference between the input and its reconstruction. 

- Assumes implicitly that pixel errors follow a Gaussian distribution. 

- Tends to produce slightly blurry outputs because it penalizes large deviations heavily and favors averaging. 

- **Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE)** 

- Used when pixel values are normalized to [0,1][0, 1] [0,1] and treated as Bernoulli probabilities. 

- More appropriate for binary or near-binary images (e.g., MNIST).

## **The re-parametrization trick** 

- Sampling is not a deterministic/differentiable process: we cannot backpropagate 

- Solution: put the random process outside the network by adding noise 

## **Issues with variational auto-encoders** 

- The decoder must output a single mean 𝜇 𝑧 

   - The output of VAEs is **blurry** 

   - Cannot be fixed by making the networks deeper 

   - it is a fundamental consequence of the Gaussian decoder objective, rooted in the least-squares nature of the reconstruction loss 

- Latent space **collapse** 

   - The decoder is often "too powerful," making it easier to generate output without using latent codes. 

   - Latent space becomes indistinguishable from the prior, causing independent and identically distributed samples 

- Possible solutions 

   - Hierarchical VAEs 

   - Vector quantized VAEs
